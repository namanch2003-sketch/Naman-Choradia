## The Story So Far

HDFC Bank was the crown jewel of Indian private banking for nearly two decades — consistently delivering 20%+ loan growth, best-in-class asset quality, and ROE above 18%. Then came the HDFC Ltd. merger in July 2023, and suddenly, this blue-chip has underperformed the Nifty Bank index by nearly 30% over eighteen months.

The question every investor is now asking: is the worst over, or is there more pain ahead?

## Why Did It Underperform?

The merger with parent HDFC Ltd. brought significant structural changes:

**1. Balance sheet compression**: The merged entity had to deal with a large, lower-yield home loan book that diluted NIMs (Net Interest Margins) from ~4.1% to ~3.4–3.5%.

**2. LDR (Loan-to-Deposit Ratio) problem**: Post-merger, LDR shot up above 110%, forcing the bank to grow deposits aggressively at higher rates — further compressing margins.

**3. Slower growth**: Management deliberately slowed loan growth to normalize the balance sheet. This disappointed markets that were accustomed to 18–20% growth from HDFC Bank.

## Signs of Stabilisation

Recent quarters show encouraging signs:

- NIM has stabilized at ~3.5% and appears to have bottomed
- LDR has declined to approximately 104% and is trending down
- Deposit growth has accelerated — CASA ratio recovering
- Gross NPA remains controlled at ~1.3%, reflecting pristine underwriting

## Valuation Argument

HDFC Bank trades at approximately 2.2x Price-to-Book (FY26E), versus its five-year historical average of 3.2–3.5x PBV. This de-rating has been warranted given the margin compression, but the margin of safety is now significant.

**Bull case**: NIM recovers to 3.8%+ over FY27, ROE returns to 17%, PBV re-rates to 3.0x → target price ~₹2,100.

**Bear case**: NIMs remain stuck at 3.4%, ROE stays subdued at ~14%, PBV at 2.0x → ₹1,500.

**Base case**: ₹1,750–1,800 over 12–18 months.

## Conclusion

For patient, long-term investors, HDFC Bank at current levels (~₹1,620) offers a compelling risk-reward. This is not a trade — it's a 2-3 year thesis about one of the best-managed banks in Asia returning to normalcy. The underperformance cycle appears to be in the final innings.

**Rating**: BUY for long-term investors. Not for traders expecting a quick re-rating.

## Business Overview

Tata Consultancy Services (TCS) is India's largest IT services company by market cap and revenue, and one of the most consistent wealth creators on Dalal Street over the past two decades. With over 600,000 employees, operations across 55+ countries, and revenues exceeding $29 billion in FY25, TCS is a genuine global enterprise.

Its business model is centred on long-duration, annuity-like contracts with Fortune 500 clients — primarily in BFSI, retail, manufacturing, and energy verticals. This gives revenue remarkable visibility and makes TCS a near-defensive play in the Indian IT space.

## Competitive Moat

TCS's moat is built on three pillars:

**1. Deep client relationships**: The company's "Land and Expand" strategy means entry contracts grow significantly over time. The number of clients contributing over $100 million annually has crossed 60 — a testament to relationship depth.

**2. Scale and delivery excellence**: TCS's global delivery model, supported by its training infrastructure (TCS iON, Ignite), ensures consistent quality at scale. Large-deal wins consistently exceed peers.

**3. Brand and trust**: In mission-critical sectors like banking and insurance, IT partners are rarely changed. TCS benefits from 30+ year relationships with many of its top clients.

Return on Equity consistently runs above 45%, with negligible debt. Free cash flow conversion exceeds 95% of net profit — a hallmark of capital efficiency.

## Valuation

At ₹3,700, TCS trades at approximately 27–28x FY26 earnings. Historically, TCS has commanded a premium multiple of 25–35x owing to its consistency and quality. At current levels, the stock is fairly valued — not cheap, but not stretched for a business of this quality.

**Fair Value Range**: ₹3,400 (bear case, 24x FY26 EPS of ₹142) to ₹4,200 (bull case, 30x FY26 EPS).

## Growth Outlook

FY26 growth is expected to recover to 8–10% in USD terms, driven by:
- Ramp-up of BSNL's large transformation deal
- Recovery in the BFSI vertical (which had been subdued due to US regional bank stress)
- AI and GenAI services adoption — TCS has 150+ active GenAI engagements

## Risks

- Prolonged slowdown in discretionary spending by US/European clients
- Rupee appreciation vs. USD
- Attrition spike if hiring picks up in the sector

## Conclusion

TCS is a core portfolio holding for long-term investors. Not a screaming buy at current levels, but a quality compounder deserving of accumulation on dips toward ₹3,300–3,400.

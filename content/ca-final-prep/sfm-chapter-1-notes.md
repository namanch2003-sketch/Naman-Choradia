## Chapter 1: Financial Risk Management — Overview

Strategic Financial Management (SFM) Paper 2 of CA Final is both feared and respected. Chapter 1 lays the conceptual groundwork for everything that follows — derivatives, forex, interest rate risk, and portfolio management. Get this right and the rest of SFM becomes significantly more tractable.

## What is Financial Risk?

Financial risk is the possibility of losing money on an investment or business venture. In SFM, we broadly classify financial risk into:

1. **Market Risk** — risk of losses due to movements in market prices (equity, interest rates, FX)
2. **Credit Risk** — risk that a counterparty will default on its obligation
3. **Liquidity Risk** — risk of being unable to exit a position without significant price impact
4. **Operational Risk** — risk of loss from failed processes, people, or systems

For exam purposes, focus primarily on market risk and its management through derivatives and hedging strategies.

## Risk vs. Uncertainty

A key distinction examiners love:

- **Risk**: Outcomes are unknown but *probabilities can be assigned* (e.g., coin toss)
- **Uncertainty**: Outcomes AND probabilities are unknown (e.g., political upheaval)

Knight's distinction (1921) is still highly relevant — risk is measurable, uncertainty is not.

## Key Risk Measures

### Standard Deviation (σ)
The most common measure of total risk. Captures how spread out returns are around the mean. Formula:

σ = √[Σ(Ri − R̄)² × Pi]

Where Ri = return in state i, R̄ = expected return, Pi = probability of state i.

### Value at Risk (VaR)
VaR answers: *"What is the maximum loss I can expect over X days with Y% confidence?"*

Example: A 1-day 95% VaR of ₹10 lakh means there is a 95% chance the loss will not exceed ₹10 lakh in a single day.

VaR methods for exam:
1. Historical simulation
2. Variance-Covariance (Parametric)
3. Monte Carlo simulation

### Beta (β)
Measures **systematic (market) risk** — the portion of risk that cannot be diversified away.

β = Cov(Ri, Rm) / Var(Rm)

- β > 1: More volatile than the market
- β < 1: Less volatile
- β = 1: Moves with the market

## Risk Management Strategies

| Strategy | Description |
|---|---|
| Avoidance | Not taking on the risky activity |
| Reduction | Diversification, hedging |
| Transfer | Insurance, derivatives |
| Acceptance | Retain risk consciously (self-insurance) |

## Examiner's Favourite Topics

- Calculation of expected return and standard deviation from probability distributions
- Portfolio risk — two-asset and multi-asset (use matrix method)
- Beta calculation and interpretation
- VaR — parametric method calculations
- Systematic vs. unsystematic risk distinction

## Study Tip

Do NOT skip theory questions in SFM. The examiner regularly awards 4–6 marks for definitions, distinctions (like risk vs. uncertainty, systematic vs. unsystematic), and conceptual explanations. These are free marks if you've read the text once.

Practice at least 3 problems on standard deviation and portfolio risk before moving to the next chapter.

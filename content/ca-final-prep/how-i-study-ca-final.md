## The Mindset First

CA Final is not a test of intelligence. It is a test of consistency, organisation, and stress management under pressure. I've seen incredibly smart people fail because they couldn't manage time during the paper — and I've seen methodical, disciplined students clear it comfortably. The exam rewards discipline, not just brilliance.

My entire prep strategy is built on one principle: **clarity over volume**. I would rather understand 70% of the syllabus deeply than skim 100% superficially.

## My Subject Priority

I split the eight subjects into three tiers:

**Tier 1 — High ROI, do first:**
- SFM (Paper 2) — high weightage, logical, formula-driven
- FR (Paper 1) — non-negotiable, huge syllabus but very scorable
- Audit (Paper 3) — writing-heavy but predictable

**Tier 2 — Moderate effort, high returns:**
- Law (Paper 4) — rote-heavy but manageable
- SCM & PE (Paper 5) — relatively straightforward

**Tier 3 — Effort-intensive, be strategic:**
- DT (Paper 7) — massive syllabus, focus on amendments
- IDT (Paper 8) — amendments-driven, practice MCQs

## Daily Schedule (Study Season)

| Time | Activity |
|---|---|
| 6:00 – 6:30 AM | Review previous day's notes (spaced repetition) |
| 6:30 – 10:30 AM | Primary study block — most cognitively demanding subject |
| 11:00 AM – 1:00 PM | Secondary subject or problem-solving |
| 2:30 – 5:30 PM | Theory review / reading |
| 6:00 – 8:00 PM | Mock tests / past papers |
| 9:00 – 10:00 PM | Light revision, next-day planning |

I take Sundays off — partially. I do 2 hours of light revision (theory, formula review) but no new topics. Rest is non-negotiable for long campaigns.

## Resource Stack

**SFM**: ICAI Study Material + ICAI Past Papers + Sanjay Saraf Sir videos for tricky topics (Options, Forex)

**FR**: ICAI Study Material is gold for FR. Supplement with Ind AS summary notes.

**Audit**: ICAI PM (Practice Manual) is the Bible. Memorise it section by section.

**DT/IDT**: Focus heavily on the amendments for the exam attempt. Use the ICAI Revision Test Papers.

## What I Avoid

- Coaching notes that summarise too aggressively — they often miss exam-critical nuances
- Studying new topics in the last two weeks before exam — revision only
- Comparing my progress with others — everyone's pace is different

## The Mock Test Discipline

Six weeks before the exam, I shift to full-length mock tests for at least three papers per week. Timing is crucial — you must simulate the 3-hour exam condition. Writing speed and answer structure are skills that only improve with practice.

The goal isn't just to pass. It's to understand finance and law well enough that it becomes a lifelong competitive advantage.

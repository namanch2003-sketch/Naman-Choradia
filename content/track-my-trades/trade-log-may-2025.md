## Overview

May 2025 was a constructive month for the markets, with Nifty recovering from the April lows and BankNifty showing early signs of a breakout. I took four trades this month — two on indices and two on individual stocks.

**Month P&L: +₹14,200 | Win Rate: 75% | Avg R:R on winners: 2.3:1**

## Trade 1 — Nifty CE (Call Option)

**Date**: May 6, 2025
**Instrument**: Nifty 23,200 CE (Weekly expiry — May 8)
**Entry**: ₹88 | **Exit**: ₹154 | **Quantity**: 50 units (1 lot)

**Rationale**: Nifty had formed a morning doji on May 5 after bouncing off the 22,900 support zone. Put-Call Ratio (PCR) had shifted above 1.2 — indicating bullish sentiment. I entered the 23,200 CE at market open on May 6 with a target of 120–150 and a stop loss of ₹55.

**Outcome**: Exited at ₹154 on expiry day as Nifty pushed to 23,380 intraday. **Profit: ₹3,300**

**Lesson**: Timing the entry at the start of the momentum candle rather than chasing was key. The trade worked because of a confluence of factors — support bounce, PCR shift, and broad market breadth improvement.

## Trade 2 — Axis Bank Futures

**Date**: May 9, 2025
**Instrument**: Axis Bank May Futures
**Entry**: ₹1,082 | **Exit**: ₹1,067 | **Quantity**: 1200 shares (1 lot)

**Rationale**: Axis Bank was testing a breakout above the ₹1,080 resistance with good volume. I entered expecting a move to ₹1,110–1,120. Stop loss was placed at ₹1,065 — below the breakout candle's low.

**Outcome**: The breakout failed. Axis Bank reversed sharply on news of higher-than-expected slippages. Stopped out at ₹1,067. **Loss: ₹18,000**

**Lesson**: Always check earnings/results calendars before entering futures trades. Axis Bank had an analyst briefing that day which I had missed. Avoidable mistake.

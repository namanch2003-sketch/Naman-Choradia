## Overview

Nifty 50 has been on a decisive recovery path since finding strong support at the 21,800 zone in mid-April. As of the third week of May 2025, the index is trading around the 23,400 mark — a critical inflection zone that will determine whether the bulls can extend the rally toward all-time highs or face a meaningful pullback.

## Trend Structure

On the weekly chart, the structure remains broadly bullish. We have a series of higher lows from the October 2023 bottom, and the 50-week EMA continues to act as a dynamic support floor. The March–April dip tested this moving average convincingly, and the subsequent recovery confirms that long-term institutional interest remains intact.

On the daily timeframe, however, the picture is more nuanced. Price is currently testing the 23,200–23,500 resistance band — a zone that acted as strong support before the Feb–March correction and has now flipped to resistance. A clean daily close above 23,500 would be technically constructive.

## Key Support Levels

- **22,800–22,900**: Immediate support. This is the breakout zone from May's initial consolidation and aligns with the 20-day EMA.
- **21,800–22,000**: Strong demand zone — the April swing low. A return here would signal a deeper correction but would still be within the bounds of the broader bullish structure.

## Key Resistance Levels

- **23,400–23,500**: The immediate ceiling. Breakout above this on volume would open the path to 24,200.
- **24,200–24,400**: The all-time high zone. Expect significant supply here on the first test.

## RSI Reading

The 14-period RSI on the daily chart is at approximately 58 — a neutral-to-bullish reading. It is not overbought, which means there is room for upside without immediate mean-reversion risk. On the weekly chart, RSI is recovering from the 45-level and heading back toward 60, consistent with a mid-cycle resumption rather than a late-cycle breakout.

## Moving Averages

The price is above its 20-day, 50-day, and 200-day SMAs — a bullish alignment. The 20-day EMA is beginning to curl upward, which is a positive short-term signal. A "golden cross" on the daily chart (50-day crossing above the 200-day) occurred in January and remains intact.

## Conclusion

The near-term bias is cautiously bullish. A breakout above 23,500 on strong breadth and volume would be a high-conviction long setup targeting 24,200. Until then, range-bound action between 22,800 and 23,500 is the base case. Risk remains to the downside if global risk-off sentiment returns.
